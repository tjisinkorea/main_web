About Me

My name is TJ and I am an American expat currently residing in Korea.

My previous working experience had me dive into the Audio Video industry, and the different clients had me explore even further in various sectors. I've gotten to work with small businesses, larger corporations, restaurants, hospitals, IT departments, pharmacies, real estate, sporting stadiums, universities, government agencies, scientists and more. I also had the pleasure of traveling the country with NASA's educational outreach team to provide technical support with their Hyperwall Lecture Series. I was fortunate enough to even witness a rocket launch!

I've gained a lot of experience in company operations, marketing, sales, logistics, event planning, product planning/launch/management, channel rep and integrator relations, and so much more. Now that I'm in a new country, I'm ready to step up to bat again to see what I can offer to a new company.

While you're here, please do take a look at my CV and let me know if you are interested in learning more about me, and how I could be a fit for your company. I work great with teams and can take leadership roles, but I am also a go-getter type that works perfectly fine alone as well.

Work experience

I spent the last four years working for Z. It was a small company that offered Audio Visual solutions at the commercial level. The CEO had downsized the company a few years back to focus on another project, and hiring me was an attempt to revitalize Z. When I joined as the Director of Sales, they had a few loyal accounts, but it was struggling.

In my first month, I landed an account with a company that built booths for tradeshows. We were to offer a turnkey solution of renting out our video wall displays and providing installation and tech support. This really opened up the door to a new rental market, and this account led to many other accounts in the same booth building sector.

I remember my second account very clearly, as it was a subsidiary of JP Morgan in the real estate market. They wanted an interactive WayFinding kiosk in each of their 6 lobbies that provided map guidance to users, as well as provide other information they wanted to convey. We provided six 40" touchscreen displays connected to Intel NUCs. I created an Amazon EC2 instance of a Windows server so that all 6 computers can be updated remotely. The hard part was creating the Wayfinding software. The next two months I spent with our engineer and designer to create maps of the buildings and creating routes, creating database of tenants, and so much more. But it was a huge sale, and even to this day an important account as they still pay monthly for server usage and support.

Up to this point our strategy was direct sales to businesses and consumers, but I decided to try to work into our strategy channel sales. I contracted manufacturer's reps from different territories and was able to snag with them their reseller/integrator contacts as well. This was a huge change because now integrators called us

I had pretty much become the spokesman of the company, dealing with all external requests, making contracts with distributors/integrators/reps, reaching out to vendors and managing key government accounts. I was also responsible for creating bids for government RFQs.

By the time I left Z, I was personally averaging $250,000 per quarter in sales, not to shabby for starting out by cold-calling with zero leads. I'm sure if COVID hadn't struck, and I hadn't left sales would have kept climbing YoY, especially because our "channel" network was becoming well established and I had just completed Z's GSA contract which listed us onto government procurement databases as a trusted manufacturer.

Because of these experience at Z, my knowledge in company operations is very rounded. I believe that this gives me an edge and also the confidence to be able to take on a variety of roles, whatever they may be, at my next opportunity. I accept and await my newest challenge!
